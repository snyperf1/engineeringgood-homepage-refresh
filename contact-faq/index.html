General

What is Engineering Good?

Engineering Good Ltd (EG) is a Singapore-registered charity that unites a diverse community of staff, volunteers, donors, sponsors, and supporters with skills in technology, engineering, and other fields. Together, we serve disadvantaged and disability communities by enabling access to digital education and economic opportunities and equipping them to overcome daily challenges through innovative humanitarian engineering solutions. Our 2 key Tech For Good programmes are:

  1. Assistive Technology (AT) for Persons with Disabilities (PwDs): EG develops technical solutions that empower PwDs to live independent and fulfilling lives. This includes the following programs:
    • Tech for Good Accelerator: This programme brings together youth innovators, tech and engineering mentors, and the disability community to collaboratively build affordable open-source AT devices and solutions.
    • Bespoke Projects: Collaborative initiatives with social service agencies and community groups to directly support PwDs with tailored AT solutions. We have a Makerspace where makers and tinkerers can access tools to create open-source AT solutions.
  2. Digital Inclusion for Vulnerable Communities: EG partners with community partners to empower disadvantaged communities by providing refurbished computers and digital literacy skills. The programmes in this area include:
    • Laptop Refurbishment and Distribution: EG refurbishes and distributes laptops to individuals who lack access, enabling them to stay digitally connected. This includes students from low-income families, displaced workers, rental flat residents, sheltered workshop workers, and migrant workers.
    • Digital Literacy Workshops: EG partners with community partners, corporate partners and volunteers to provide relevant digital literacy lessons for vulnerable communities.

Digital Inclusion (DI)

What can I donate?

Thank you for your interest in donating your devices!

We take in working laptops that feature processors which are eligible for upgrade to Windows 11 following Microsoft’s announcement on the End of support for Windows 10. Please refer to Microsoft’s list Read more about We take in working laptops that feature processors which are eligible for upgrade to. for the list of supported processors.

Each device should come as a complete set with at least the following specs:

  • Windows laptops
    • Processor: 8th Gen Intel (e.g. i5-8350U, i7-8650U)
  • Batteries (internal or external)
    • No bloated batteries
    • 60% battery capacity
  • RAM
    • 16GB
  • Storage
    • 256GB SSDs
    • No HDDs
  • Keyboard
    • All keys must work
    • No missing keycaps or faded keys
  • Webcam
    • No scratches on lens
  • Chargers
    • Original working charger
  • Aesthetic condition
    • Minimal scratches on body
    • No dents on body or ports

Cash donations are greatly appreciated to keep our programmes running and the office lights on!

If you are an organisation and would like to donate laptops, do write to contactus@engineeringgood.org.

EG reserves the right to, at its sole discretion, refurbish, distribute, repurpose, recycle, e-waste or realise the residual value of donated devices and/or accessories that subsidise its operational costs in order to execute its charitable mission to the fullest extent. How do I donate laptops to Engineering Good?

The short: Prepare an asset list and email contactus@engineeringgood.org, then drop your laptops and chargers at our office during weekdays (10am – 6pm).

The long: Kindly prepare an asset list containing the information such as laptop brand, model, and specs. We seek your understanding to pack the laptops and chargers into cardboard boxes and deliver them to us as we do not have the resources to arrange delivery.

I am not sure if I should donate a defective laptop. Do you accept faulty sets?

Apologies, we do not have the space to accumulate things for repair. As such, we recommend the following:

  1. Dispose responsibly at e-waste sites. Refer to NEA for more info.
  2. DIY repair can be an option! The internet is full of resources and a really great teacher.
  3. List your device on online platforms/social media. Someone might want it!
How can I receive laptops from Engineering Good?

Engineering Good does not usually distribute refurbished laptops directly to individuals. Instead, we work through our network of community partners. Here are some organisations that you can approach: https://www.msf.gov.sg/our-services/directories#familytab.

Alternatively, do contact your family service centre or social service office to write to us. We will work with them and get the laptops to you.

I am from a social service agency/non-profit, how do I request laptops for my service users?

We have a simple two-step process!

  1. Read our Notice for Community Partners. 
  2. Send us the signed Waiver form and Request table to request4laptops@engineeringgood.org.

Assistive Technology (AT)

What is Tech For Good (T4G) festival?

Tech For Good (T4G) is an annual innovation challenge organised by Engineering Good. It is the only festival in Singapore that gathers youth innovator, tech professionals, and the disability community to create affordable, open-source assistive technology solutions for the disability community.
EG ran the festival over 5 years.

The theme for 2023 was inclusive employment held at the National Library @ Bugis featuring an inclusive marketplace, talks, performances, and more!

Visit the T4G 2023 website for more info on our past runs.
For 2024 – 2025, EG will focus on further developing past prototypes that emerged from the T4G festivals through our new programme: Tech For Good Accelerator! (Working in progress, please tahan!)

What is a makerspace? A workbench type area with tools and materials for ideas and concepts to be prototyped, tested, iterated, documented, and shared. Makers who use the space are creative, innovative, and learn by doing together. What is EG's Inclusive Makerspace?

The Makerspace at Engineering Good focuses on tech for social good. Makers and projects that implement technology (digital or otherwise) to meet the needs of persons with disabilities are most welcome to use the space to develop concepts and prototypes. No prior experience is necessary for participation. However a willingness to learn from others and share knowledge and skills is a prerequisite. Diversity of skills and experience is most welcome. You don’t have to be a maker to work with makers.

Can anyone use EG's Inclusive Makerspace?

Definitely! However, makers are required to build prototypes that support the disability community.

We’re open to new visitors on Sundays between 11am and 6pm. Prior registration is required to comply with safe distancing guidelines. Please email help@engineeringgood.org with your intended date and time of visit so that we can manage the capacity. What are the tools available at EG's Makerspace?
  • Hand tools (screwdrivers, pliers, hammers, and similar) 
  • Power tools (drill, saw, sanding, and similar)
  • Soldering (electronics and repair)
  • Microcontrollers prototyping (Arduino, Micro:Bit, and similar)
  • Digital solutions and Internet of Things “IoT”(RaspberryPi, Alexa, etc)
  • 3D printer (Prusa i3 mk3)
